Indonesia EV Market Report:
Q1 2026 Deep Dive

How a tightened TKDN regime, the PMK 12/2025 incentive recalibration, and shifting Chinese-brand strategies reshaped Indonesia's electric vehicle market in the first three months of 2026.

§ Executive Summary

The headline finding.

Indonesia's EV market entered Q1 2026 in a structurally different shape than it left Q4 2025. The PMK 12/2025 incentive recalibration narrowed the gap between locally assembled and fully imported electric vehicles, while the TKDN local-content threshold quietly forced brands to disclose more about where their batteries and powertrains actually come from.

For the brands that had front-loaded their CKD investments, this was a tailwind. For the brands still relying on CBU imports for their core volume, Q1 brought visible compression in market share. The headline number — Chinese brands now account for an outright majority of new EV registrations — masks a more interesting story underneath: the gap between brand-level performance widened sharply, with the top three pulling further ahead of everyone else.

§ Key Findings

Four signals to watch.

// 01 · Market share

Chinese brands cross the majority threshold

53% of registrations

For the first time in a single quarter, Chinese-origin EV brands accounted for more than half of all new electric vehicle registrations in Indonesia, with the top three brands driving most of that share.

// 02 · Regulation

PMK 12/2025 recalibrates the incentive map

The revised PPnBM treatment narrowed the price advantage previously enjoyed by CBU imports, pushing brands without local assembly to either invest in CKD or accept a structurally smaller addressable market.

// 03 · YoY performance

The middle of the leaderboard hollows out

+118% YoY top 3

While the top three brands grew triple-digits year on year, brands ranked 4–8 saw flat-to-negative volume change. The market is not just growing — it's actively concentrating.

// 04 · Regional

Permendagri 11/2026 reshapes the PKB / BBNKB picture

Updated regional tax frameworks under Permendagri 11/2026 changed the total cost of EV ownership in several key provinces, shifting where Q2 demand is likely to concentrate geographically.

§ Methodology & Sources

How this was put together.

Data sources

  • GAIKINDO monthly registration data (Q1 2026)
  • PMK 12/2025 (Ministry of Finance regulation)
  • Permendagri 11/2026 (Ministry of Home Affairs regulation)
  • Brand-level press releases and investor reports
  • Industry trade press for context and verification

Approach

Brand-level registration figures were collected for Q1 2026 and compared against the same period in 2025 to produce YoY change. Market share was calculated against total quarterly EV registrations. Regulatory framing draws on primary regulatory texts cross-referenced with industry commentary; nothing was paraphrased through secondary sources.

Scope & caveats

This report covers battery-electric passenger vehicles only. Hybrid (HEV / PHEV) and commercial-segment EVs are excluded. Figures are based on registrations rather than retail sales — the two can differ in any given month due to inventory timing.
